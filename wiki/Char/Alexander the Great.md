## **Early Life**

Alexander was born in 356 BC in **Pella**, the capital of Macedonia. He was the son of **King Philip II of Macedonia** and **Queen Olympias**. Tutored by the philosopher **Aristotle**, Alexander developed a lifelong love for learning, philosophy, and science, which influenced his approach to leadership and governance.

## **Rise to Power**

After the assassination of his father in 336 BC, Alexander ascended to the Macedonian throne at the age of 20. He quickly consolidated power by eliminating rivals and suppressing rebellions in Greece. His ambition extended beyond Macedonia, aiming to conquer the **Persian Empire**, the dominant power of the time.

---

## **Military Campaigns and Conquests**

### **Conquest of Asia Minor**
- [**Battle of Granicus (334 BC)**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Granicus): Alexander’s first major victory against the Persian satraps of Asia Minor.

- [**Siege of Halicarnassus (334 BC)**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Halicarnassus): Captured a key coastal city, demonstrating strategic brilliance.

### **Defeat of the Persian Empire**

- [**Battle of Issus (333 BC)**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Issus): Defeated Persian King **Darius III**, securing control over Syria and the eastern Mediterranean.

- [**Conquest of Egypt (332 BC)**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_the_Great#Conquest_of_Egypt): Welcomed as a liberator; founded the city of **Alexandria**, which became a cultural and economic hub.

- [**Battle of Gaugamela (331 BC)**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Gaugamela): Decisive victory that led to the fall of the Persian Empire.

### **Campaign in India**

- [**Battle of the Hydaspes (326 BC)**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Hydaspes): Defeated King **Porus**, extending his empire into the Indian subcontinent.

- Faced resistance from his own troops and eventually turned back, marking the eastern limit of his conquests.

---

## **Death**

Alexander died in **Babylon in 323 BC** at the age of 32, under mysterious circumstances—possibly fever, poisoning, or other causes. His sudden death left his empire without a strong successor, leading to its fragmentation among his generals, known as the **Diadochi**.

---
## **Personal Life and Relationships**

- Alexander is widely believed to have had romantic and intimate relationships with both men and women.

- Most historians agree that his most significant male companion was **Hephaestion**, his close friend and general. Ancient sources suggest a deep emotional and possibly romantic bond between them.

- He married **Roxana**, a Bactrian princess, and later **Stateira II** and **Parysatis II** as part of political alliances.

- His sexuality reflects the norms of Macedonian and Greek culture at the time, where same-sex relationships among men, especially among the elite, were not uncommon.
---

## **Legacy**

- Spread **Hellenistic culture** across three continents.
    
- Founded **over 20 cities**, many named Alexandria.
    
- His military tactics are studied in modern military academies.
    
- Inspired leaders such as **Julius Caesar** and **Napoleon Bonaparte**.
    
- Remembered as a symbol of ambition, vision, and leadership.
